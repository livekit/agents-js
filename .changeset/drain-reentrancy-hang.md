Fix a drain deadlock that hangs the agent when a sub-task (`AgentTask`) completes via a tool-only turn that races a user interruption. `AgentTask.run()`'s `finally` resumes the parent via `_updateActivity(..., 'resume')` → `drain()` → `_pauseSchedulingTask()`, which awaits `_mainTask.result`. The mainTask cannot reach its drain loop-exit until its pending speech tasks de-register from `speechTasks`, so awaiting its result deadlocks in two cases: (1) the drain runs from inside one of the activity's own in-flight speech tasks (the completing turn) — a self-await; and (2) a barge-in cascade leaves the drain running from a non-reentrant context while the mainTask is held only by "zombie" speech tasks — already done, or whose handle was interrupted by the cascade — that have not de-registered yet. Either way the parent never resumes and the agent goes silent until the participant disconnects. `_pauseSchedulingTask` now skips the self-await when invoked reentrantly from one of the activity's own speech tasks, or when every pending drain speech task is already done or interrupted; the mainTask is still reaped by the subsequent `close()` → `cancelAndWait`. Fixes #836.
